On a Personal Note

Published on May 2, 2022
Updated on May 2, 2022

Last few words grabbed from book just finished ‘How the ThinkPad changed the World and is Shaping the Future’ - Arimasa Naitoh & William J. Holstein

Having been diagnosed with lung cancer six years ago,
and undergoing a painful operation that appears to have removed it all,
I have a plenty of time to contemplate questions of mortality.
I think back to my father.
He was just a regular citizen.
He was not famous in any way.
But without my recognizing it, he taught me how to think and how to solve problems that peple face.
He helped me define an emotional center.
He shaped my behaviour.
As a result, he was still alive in me long after he was phisically gone from this world.

If I have been successful in trasferring my knowledge,
basic behaviours and style to my own childern and to my young engineers,
that’s all I ask for.
That’s how I want to close out my life.
I don’t care if people remember my name.
I don’t care whether they recognize that I contributed to their thought processes.
If the spirit of my life’s work is transferred to the next generation, to my army of innovators,
I’ll still be there.
